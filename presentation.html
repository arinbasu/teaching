Skills required to use a software tool is not taught in Epidemiology/health sciences courses

Software Carpentry

  • Goal is to teach Competence in Computing
  • Started with the idea that scientists can do productive work if they learned how to compute
  • Short focused trainings are better than week long training
  • Peer helpers and live coding with frequent feedbacks while teaching helps to reinforce ideas ( For a comprehensive article, see )

Software carpentry in action

Live coding (Will bring up a youtube window)

What was done¶

  • Students were provided a challenge to solve a critical appraisal topic
  • Students brought their own laptops to work
  • Instructor (the presenter) used his own laptop in the classroom
  • Students and the instructor worked together in a live coding session
  • Students were encourage to ask questions as they got stuck
  • The version of "live coding" was to work in the same room working on the same problem
  • Students worked in "pairs" that helped each other

Lessons learned¶

  • It was modelled after software carpentry principles to merge critical appraisal with computing
  • Students needed hand holding
  • A web app for critical appraisal was useful
  • The grades of the students improved compared with demonstration alone
  • Verbal feedback from students suggested they liked this format
  • This strategy reduced the need for one on one sessions
